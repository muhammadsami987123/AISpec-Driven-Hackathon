# Feature Specification: AI-native-bot Documentation: Chapter 4 - Advanced Interaction Patterns

## 1. Overview
This feature involves researching and writing Chapter 4 of the AI-native-bot documentation. Chapter 4 will focus on advanced interaction patterns and practical applications of AI in bot development, building upon the foundational knowledge established in previous chapters. The goal is to provide accurate, relevant, and original content that maintains the existing tone, formatting, and depth of the documentation, with 5 main topics.

## 2. Scope
### In Scope
*   Researching the logical progression of AI-native bot documentation after foundational chapters (assuming Chapters 1-3 covered basics, architecture, and core functionalities).
*   Defining 5 main topics for Chapter 4 that fit the "Advanced Interaction Patterns" theme.
*   Writing original content for each of the 5 topics.
*   Ensuring the content is accurate, relevant, and maintains the existing tone, formatting (markdown), and depth of the documentation.
*   Integration of code examples or conceptual diagrams if they enhance understanding without requiring actual hardware.
### Out of Scope
*   Implementing any actual bot code or features.
*   Creating hands-on lab exercises requiring actual hardware (per clarification document).
*   Publishing or deploying the documentation.
*   Designing UI components (per `ui-designer-chatkit` agent description).

## 4. Functional Requirements
List detailed, testable requirements for the feature.
- FR1: Chapter 4 shall present 5 distinct main topics related to advanced interaction patterns or practical AI applications in bots.
- FR2: Each main topic shall have a clear, professional heading.
- FR3: The content for each topic shall be concise, professional, accurate, and original.
- FR4: The chapter's tone and style shall be consistent with previous chapters of the AI-native-bot documentation.
- FR5: The formatting of Chapter 4 shall adhere to markdown standards and match the existing documentation.
- FR6: The depth of explanation shall be consistent with the level of detail provided in previous chapters, suitable for an intermediate to advanced audience.

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements
Describe performance, security, reliability, and other quality attributes.
- NFR1: **Readability**: Content should be easy to understand for readers familiar with basic AI and bot concepts.
- NFR2: **Accuracy**: All technical information presented must be factually correct.
- NFR3: **Consistency**: The chapter must maintain a consistent narrative flow and terminology with the rest of the book.

Define measurable outcomes that determine the feature's success.
