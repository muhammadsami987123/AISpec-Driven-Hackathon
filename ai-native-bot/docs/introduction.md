### What is Physical AI?

Physical AI represents a transformative frontier in artificial intelligence, focusing on systems that operate within and understand the complexities of the real physical world. Unlike traditional, purely digital AI, Physical AI systems possess a physical body, enabling them to directly interact with their surroundings, gather sensory data, and execute actions that have tangible effects. This bridges the critical gap between abstract digital intelligence and the concrete realities of gravity, friction, and spatial awareness.

#### Definition & Historical Context

Physical AI is defined as AI systems that function in reality and comprehend physical laws through embodied interaction. This concept emphasizes the integration of AI with a physical form, allowing for real-time learning and adaptation based on sensory experiences and motor outputs.

### Why 2025 is the Breakthrough Year

The year 2025 is heralded as a breakthrough for Physical AI due to a remarkable convergence of technological advancements including computational power (modern GPUs and edge devices), sensor maturation (cameras, LiDAR, depth sensors), advanced AI algorithms (deep learning, RL, LLMs), robust open-source ecosystems (ROS 2, Gazebo, Isaac Sim), and large-scale investment into robotics.
